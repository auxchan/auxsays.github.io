## Why this matters

A lot of creators do not mind paying for good software. The problem is not paying. The problem is building a workflow around a tool that presents itself as free, then blocks a critical export step once you are already invested.

This article is built around a simple question:

> What can you actually do for free, and when should you leave for a cleaner workflow?

## Good rule of thumb

If a platform controls your final export quality, licensing, or watermark status, treat that as a risk point from day one.

## Practical next steps

- Keep original design assets whenever possible
- Prefer workflows you can reproduce without a closed export gate
- Maintain a list of fallback tools before a project depends on one platform

## Better article angle for readers

Instead of trying to force a locked workflow to do something it does not want to do, show people:

- what the real free tier covers
- where the restrictions start
- what legal alternatives are better
- how to preserve scale and flexibility upstream
